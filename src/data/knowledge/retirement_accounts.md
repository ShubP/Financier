# Retirement and Tax-Advantaged Accounts

## Why account type matters

Where you hold investments affects how they are taxed, which changes
how much you keep. Tax-advantaged retirement accounts let your money
grow with taxes deferred or even tax-free, which compounds into a large
advantage over a career.

## 401(k) and workplace plans

A **401(k)** is an employer-sponsored plan. Contributions are often
deducted from your paycheck before taxes (traditional) and grow tax
deferred until withdrawal. Many employers **match** part of your
contribution — for example, 50% of the first 6% you contribute. That
match is an immediate, guaranteed return, so contributing at least
enough to capture the full match is usually a top priority.

## IRAs

An **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)** is one you open yourself.

- **Traditional IRA / 401(k):** contributions may be tax-deductible
  now; you pay ordinary income tax on withdrawals in retirement.
- **Roth IRA / Roth 401(k):** you contribute after-tax dollars now, and
  qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free, including all the
  growth.

## Traditional vs. Roth — the core trade-off

The choice hinges on when you expect your tax rate to be higher. If you
think your tax rate will be higher in retirement (or you are early in
your career), Roth can be attractive because you lock in today's rate.
If you expect a lower rate later, traditional defers tax to that
cheaper time. Many people split between the two to hedge.

## Contribution limits and rules

These accounts have annual contribution limits and rules about
withdrawals before retirement age, which can trigger taxes and
penalties. Limits change periodically, so check current figures. The
account is just a container — you still choose investments (often
low-cost index funds) inside it.

## A common priority order

1. Contribute enough to a 401(k) to get the full employer match.
2. Build an emergency fund.
3. Add to an IRA (Roth or traditional) and/or more 401(k).
4. Invest beyond that in a regular taxable brokerage account.
