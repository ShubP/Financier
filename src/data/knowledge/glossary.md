# Glossary of Common Financial Terms

- **Asset allocation:** how a portfolio is divided among stocks, bonds,
  cash, and other asset classes.
- **Bear market:** a prolonged decline in prices, often defined as a
  drop of 20% or more from a recent peak.
- **Bull market:** a sustained period of rising prices.
- **Bond:** a loan to a government or company that pays interest and
  returns principal at maturity.
- **Capital gain:** the profit from selling an asset for more than you
  paid.
- **Compound interest:** earning returns on both your principal and on
  previously earned returns.
- **Diversification:** spreading investments so no single holding can
  dominate the outcome.
- **Dividend:** a share of company profits paid to shareholders.
- **Dollar-cost averaging:** investing a fixed amount on a regular
  schedule regardless of price.
- **ETF:** an exchange-traded fund that holds a basket of securities
  and trades like a stock.
- **Expense ratio:** a fund's annual fee as a percent of assets
  invested.
- **Index fund:** a fund that tracks a market index at low cost.
- **Inflation:** the gradual rise in prices that erodes purchasing
  power over time.
- **Liquidity:** how quickly an asset can be converted to cash without
  a large price hit.
- **Maturity:** the date a bond repays its principal.
- **Portfolio:** the complete collection of your investments.
- **Principal:** the original amount invested or borrowed.
- **Rebalancing:** restoring a portfolio to its target allocation.
- **Risk tolerance:** how much volatility an investor can comfortably
  endure.
- **Roth account:** a retirement account funded with after-tax money;
  qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
- **Volatility:** the degree to which an investment's price fluctuates.
- **Yield:** the income an investment produces, as a percent of price.
